CONCEPT PANE — WHAT IS LIQUIDITY?
The Core Idea — Liquidity Is Just Resting Orders
Every time price makes an equal high (EQH) or an old swing high, retail traders place buy stops just above it (breakout buyers) and sellers place stop-losses just above it too. That cluster of orders is "buy-side liquidity" — a magnet.

The same happens below equal lows (EQL) and old swing lows — that's "sell-side liquidity".

Big players need this liquidity to fill their own large orders without moving price too much against themselves. So price gets engineered to sweep through these pools before reversing.

This is why your SL got hit right before the real move last time — it was sitting inside sell-side liquidity.

Liquidity Types — Quick Reference
Liquidity Type	Description	Tag
Equal Highs (EQH)	2+ highs at nearly the same level	Buy-side pool (red)
Equal Lows (EQL)	2+ lows at nearly the same level	Sell-side pool (green)
Old swing high/low	Unmitigated, untested level	Magnet (amber)
Round numbers	24,000 / 24,100 etc	Psychological liquidity (blue)
Session highs/lows	Asian, London, NY session extremes	Strong magnet (amber)
Visual Concept — Liquidity Above and Below Price

Sell-side liquidity zone: Area below equal lows where resting sell stops are clustered

Price moves between these zones, sweeping liquidity pools before reversing direction. Smart money hunts these pools to fill their large orders.

This tells you roughly where a sweep is likely — but this is NOT where your SL goes.

Step 2 — Wait for the 1-Min Sweep + Rejection Wick + CHoCH
Let the hunt actually happen first. Patience here is what separates this from the previous trade.

Step 3 — Place SL a Few Points Below the Actual Sweep Wick Low
Add a small buffer (5-10 points on NIFTY) beyond the exact wick. This is now beyond the liquidity hunt zone, not inside it.

Trade-Off to Accept
Waiting for the sweep to complete means you enter a bit later and your SL is a bit further from entry than chasing the breakout. That's fine — adjust position size down slightly to keep risk the same 2%. A smaller, later, safer entry beats a bigger one that gets hunted out.

DAILY ROUTINE PANE — COMPLETE MORNING PROCESS
Before Market Open (9:00-9:15 AM)
Open 15-min chart. Mark prior day high/low, weekly high/low, major EQH/EQL, weak highs/lows. This is your liquidity map for the day.

First 30-45 Minutes (9:15-10:00 AM)
Switch to 5-min. Watch which direction structure is breaking relative to your 15-min map. Identify the nearest 5-min OB/demand/supply zone in line with your bias.

As Price Approaches the Zone
Drop to 1-min. Watch for the liquidity sweep — a wick through the zone that rejects. Wait for 1-min CHoCH to confirm.

Entry
Enter on the confirmation candle after CHoCH. SL goes below the sweep wick (1-min), not at the round 5-min/15-min level.

Management
Book 50% at first 5-min target (next OB or POC). Trail remainder using 15-min liquidity pools as final targets.

DAILY PRACTICE CHECKLIST
Mark this on your chart every day for 2 weeks to build the habit:

Marked prior day high/low + weekly high/low on 15-min

Identified all EQH/EQL clusters with 3+ touches

Marked 5-min OB zones in line with 15-min bias

Waited for 1-min sweep wick before entering, not before

Placed SL below sweep wick, not at the obvious level

Reviewed end of day — which liquidity pools got swept, which didn't
